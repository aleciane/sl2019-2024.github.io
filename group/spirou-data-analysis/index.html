*Thread Reply:* et la methode dans le Cook+2022 pour les raies telluriques en absorption: The detailed performances of SPIROU’s telluric correction will be presented in a forthcoming publication (É. Artigau 2022, in preparation); we summarize the main steps here. Most telluric-correction schemes are either model-based (e.g., Molec- fit, Smette et al. 2015 or Allart et al. 2022) or purely empirical (Artigau et al. 2014; Bedell et al. 2019). Catherine Dougados (catherine.dougados@univ-grenoble-alpes.fr)

Et sinon dans le papier de Cook 2022 PASP: The SPIROU detector control software reads the detector continuously every 5.57s and produces a 2D image (4096 × 4096) constructed from the linear fit of the pixel value versus time (henceforth the slope, as well as an intercept, error, and number of frames used for quality checks—see Section 4.5). This is the raw 2D “ramp” image used by APERO as an initial input. An overview of this can be found in Appendix A but this software is not provided as part of APERO (but the raw cubes are stored by CFHT). The “ramp” images are supplied by CFHT (via CADC21) and are thus referred to as the raw images for input into APERO.

I remain on the fence about this FAP business. I’ve run a few tests and, depending on the SNR of the feature, one can get chi2 or chi2_red to remain essentially unchanged while binning. If thew FAP calculation takes into account not just chi2 and the number of points, but also the likelihood that a (smooth, correlated) signal is present, then I am happy to accept that it gets better while binning. My main source of concern is that the decision on whether a signal is present or not based on a chi2 can only be robustly made with more info than just the data (e.g., if one knows the shape of the signal, then you can improved by using a match filter). With data and uncertainty alone, the quantity of information at hand is identical whether binning or not, which was the position I was stuck on this morning

And it would be nice to be able to test whether the two populations are sampling the same HI Brg fluxes or not. The problem of deriving the K band magnitude from the spectral type and the veiling is that in Class I I suspect that much (if not all) of the K band magnitude is affected by scattering. So that most likely we are only receiving at K-band a small fraction of the true K band flux emitted by the protostar.
