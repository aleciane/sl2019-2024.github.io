*Thread Reply:* Very interesting, sorry to have missed the talk. Wondering whether you can derive the rotational broadening, i.e., vsini, from the photospheric spectrum of Class 0 sources ? Of course, one of the difficulties is that if this is scattered light from the poles (i.e., base of the polar cavity, and not direct light from the protostellar photosphere), it is as if the observer were looking at the star pole-on (independent of the actual inclination of the system). Hence, sini <<1, and a small vsini would not necessarily imply a low rotation. Same applies to FWHM, could it be the reason why the FWHM of Class 0 lines are systematically lower than those of Class I?

*Thread Reply:* Hi Jerome, I didn’t see your reply, which explains my late response (being in California, I also missed several discussions of the workshop, and Slack threads…). The modeling of the photospheric absorption lines of these Class 0 protostars is on-going work, and I cannot conclude firmly right now on the typical rotational broadening we obtain --- I will update this group about this work. The inclination is definitely a parameter I will try to constrain with follow-up ALMA high angular resolution observations of these objects (with disk dust thermal and gas emission, as well as outflow tracers). Eventually all of this will become clear with these future studies and data. Happy to discuss more about it.
