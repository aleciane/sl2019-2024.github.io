Hello everyone, @channel When looking more carefully at the magnetic signatures, there are two objects for which it makes much more sense to perform a magnetic monitoring : IRAS 04113+2458S (aka MHO 1), and V806 Tau. In the other sources, the magnetic signatures are only detected once, and many are marginals. MHO 1 is an M2 flat-spectrum source, and V806 Tau is a G7 flat-spectrum source. For our objectives in PROMETHEE I would favour MHO 1 for the following reasons: • MHO 1 has a similar temperature than V347 Aur • V347 Aur is the only protostar in which we have monitored the magnetic fields, and we find a very strange magnetic topology: almost entirely toroidal with a low magnetic intensity (<100 G). Such a behaviour would need to be confirmed in similar objects. • MHO 1 would therefore be a good candidate to observe and to compare to V347 Aur • The magnetic field in MHO 1 appears to be much stronger than in V347 Aur • Its magnetic signature is changing on timescales of days, indicating that we are not observing it pole-on, or that the B-axis and rotation-axis are not aligned. This is an important condition in order to be able to perform ZDI (we see the magnetic topology changing as the star rotates) • The Zeeman signatures changes definitely over one day, but not too much, which indicates that the rotation period is not small, but it also indicates that within one SPIRou run we should be able to retrieve the rotation period, and map the magnetic field at the same time, if we get one observation per night over 15 nights.
